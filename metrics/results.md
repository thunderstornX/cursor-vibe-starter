### Reading these numbers

The breakdown reflects what this repo *is*: a methodology repo
where the prompt library and the test suite are hand-authored prose
and code (most of `[manual]`), the worked-example service was built
collaboratively (most of `[mixed]`), and the boilerplate-heavy
middleware + routes + Dockerfile were generated under the
`.cursorrules` contract and reviewed (most of `[ai]`).

A different project — say, a domain-heavy backend — would produce a
different shape: more `[ai]` for cookie-cutter CRUD, more `[manual]`
for the bits of business logic that don't have a template to lean
on. The protocol is the same; the shape varies. That's the point.

## How to read these numbers

- **Net LOC** is `insertions - deletions`. A commit that rewrites a
  function in place reports a small net even though the diff is
  large.
- **% of net** is share of net LOC across `[ai]`, `[manual]`, `[mixed]`,
  and `[untagged]`. `[merge]` and `[tooling]` are excluded from the
  share calculation; both contain little to no original content per
  the protocol.
- **A high `untagged` share is a smell.** It means the discipline
  drifted; the value of the report dropped accordingly. If you see a
  release with > 5% untagged, ask the author to amend the next
  release notes with the bucket.

## Why this is a *productivity record*, not a *productivity proof*

The single number readers usually want — "how much faster did AI
make you?" — isn't here. It can't be: I didn't build this codebase
twice (once with AI, once without) and time both runs. What this
report can answer is more modest: **of the lines of code shipped in
this release, what share came through which workflow?** That's a
record, not a controlled experiment, and it's the strongest claim
the data can support.
